                    <h3>1. The Postulates of Quantum Mechanics</h3>
                    <p>At the university level, quantum mechanics is built upon a set of rigorous mathematical postulates. The state of a physical system is represented by a <a href="/physics/subtopic/state-vector" class="subtopic-link"><strong>State Vector</strong></a> (\( |\psi\rangle \)) in a complex vector space. Every observable physical quantity (such as position, momentum, or energy) corresponds to a <a href="/physics/subtopic/hermitian-operators" class="subtopic-link"><strong>Hermitian Operator</strong></a>. The possible outcomes of a measurement are the eigenvalues of these operators, and the state of the system "collapses" into the corresponding eigenvector upon observation.</p>
